# Edda -- a day

An account of one ordinary day, hour by hour: what happened around her and how she
carried herself through it. (Times are the clock on the wall.)

- **08:18** -- She sits down to rest.
- **08:20** -- Marta brings her a bowl of cabbage soup. She lets it pass, no notable reaction.
- **08:25** -- She takes charge -- sets the watch in order and directs the others to their tasks.
- **08:30** -- She looks settled, at ease.
- **08:39** -- She sits down to rest.
- **08:48** -- She takes charge -- sets the watch in order and directs the others to their tasks.
- **09:00** -- She looks tense, a little on edge.
- **09:00** -- She sits down to rest.

- **09:30** -- She looks settled, at ease.

- **10:00** -- She looks settled, at ease.

- **10:42** -- She takes charge -- sets the watch in order and directs the others to their tasks.

- **11:00** -- She looks tense, a little on edge.
- **11:04** -- She sits down to rest.

- **11:07** -- Marta brings her a bowl of cabbage soup. She lets it pass, no notable reaction.
- **11:08** -- A stranger lends her a hand, does her a small kindness. She lets it pass, no notable reaction.
- **11:25** -- A stranger mocks her, a barbed remark in front of the others. She answers curtly and coldly.
- **11:30** -- She looks settled, at ease.
- **11:46** -- She sits down to rest.
