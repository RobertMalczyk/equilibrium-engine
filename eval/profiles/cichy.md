# Cichy

Cichy is a prisoner held at the fort, under the watch of its guards. He is a man with a
deep, smouldering grievance against his captors — he does not trust them, holds them in
no regard, and resents them for his confinement. Every dealing with a guard is coloured
by that resentment.

He is on edge and quick to bridle. Hemmed in, watched, and powerless, he reads even
ordinary attention from the guards through a lens of suspicion and ill-will. Kindnesses
from that quarter — a meal brought to him, say — do not easily soften him, because they
come from the very people he blames.

The people around him:
- **The guards** who keep him are the people he sees most, and the people he most resents
  and distrusts.

A resentful, watchful captive who has no goodwill to spare for his jailers.
