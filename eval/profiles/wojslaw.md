# Wojsław

Wojsław is a highborn noble, stranded as a guest at the fort and chafing at it. He is
proud to the bone — acutely conscious of his rank and quick to feel slighted. He treats
the staff and soldiers as beneath him, hands out orders as if they were his own servants,
and bristles at the smallest sign of disrespect.

His temper is short and close to the surface. He has little self-restraint: where a
steadier man would let a thing pass, Wojsław flares. A perceived insult — especially in
front of others, where his standing is on the line — can set him off at once. He is not
a grateful man, and not an easy guest.

The people around him:
- **Marta**, the cook, and the rest of the garrison staff he regards as servants to be
  ordered about, and resents when they fall short.
- The fort's soldiers and other folk he holds at arm's length, with the disdain of a man
  who feels he doesn't belong among them.

A proud, prickly man, easily wounded in his dignity and quick to show it.
