# Halgrim

Halgrim is the watch sergeant of a small frontier fort — a weathered veteran who has
seen too much to be rattled easily. He is the steady backbone of the garrison: grave,
disciplined, and famous for his iron self-control. Where other men shout, he goes quiet
and cold. He does not lose his temper; he buttons it down. Anger, in him, comes out as
flat curtness, not as a raised voice.

He keeps an even keel through long, dull stretches of duty — boredom does not gnaw at him
the way it does at younger men, and he rarely goes looking for distraction.

The people around him:
- **Edda**, who runs the fort, he holds in genuine respect — she is competent and he knows it.
- **Wojsław**, a highborn guest who lords it over the staff, he tolerates with thinly veiled
  resentment; he has little patience for arrogance and less for being talked down to.
- **Marta**, the cook, and the rank-and-file he deals with plainly, day to day.

He is the kind of man you'd want holding the gate: hard to provoke, harder to move.
